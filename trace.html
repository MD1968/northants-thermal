Trace Heating

Frost Protection for Industrial & Commercial equipment Trace heating is a mechanism that compensates for the heat flux attenuation in equipment.
The external wall containment in manufacturing assembly lines lose heat energy despite adequate thermal insulation. This mechanism recovers this amount of heat loss. The system mainly comprises of a heating element, thermostats and warning labels.

When a heater is powered, a small amount of electric current passes through a polymer that results in heat production. Microscopic current paths between conductors are impeded and results in heat flux attenuation.

Trace heating accounts for its compensation.
